What is a study group?

A group of people who are in the same program They come together to learn/review material They might meet regularly or only before exams They meet to learn about the material, not to finish a project

Why study in groups?

To share knowledge and resources Some people learn better with others Having study groups can force you to stay on top of the material It helps build skills such as organization, leadership, and cooperation Encourages working together instead of competition It’s fun and social

Different Approaches to Study Groups

Informal Study Groups

Easy! Get together with your notes

  • Students get together and either go through class material or review questions
  • Advantages: no real prep required; students can share ideas and knowledge
  • Disadvantages: may not be as productive; students may not be prepared to help one another or may give incorrect information
Structured Study Groups

Harder! Let's talk more about how to do this.

  • Students decide what they would like to go over in advance so that they can prepare
  • The group may even assign tasks or parts of the material to each person to be the expert
  • Advantages: More productive; everyone is prepared and makes a contribution
  • Disadvantages:More effort required

The Planning Stage

  • Establish goals- what do people want to get out of the sessions? Who should be involved?
  • Determine how you would like to approach the material: teach or review the material or come up with study questions.
  • It can help either assign someone the task of coordinating or get together to divide up the work
  • Ensure that everyone has something to do
  • Decide on a meeting time and place: think about where you will work best (avoid noise and distractions) and bring appropriate materials

Study Strategies

STUDY STRATEGY #1

Predicting Test/Exam Questions.Have each person come up with study questions from class. You can also use a test or reviews questions in a textbook.

STUDY STRATEGY #2

Summarize.Come up with a list of terms and concepts. Take turns having people summarize this in their own words. Have each person write down their definition, then read the actual definition.

STUDY STRATEGY #3

Keeping it fun!Some ideas for games can include: matching terms and definitions, speed labelling, quizzing (or jeopardy), and finding interactive games online.

STUDY STRATEGY #4

Use white-boards, projectors, and study spaces.You can use spaces and equipment on campus. Check out the Algonquin College blog to find study spaces on campus.

STUDY STRATEGY #5

Come up with memory tools to share.Figure out what group members are having trouble with remembering. Divide these concepts up and have each person come up with a memory tool, a song, a rhyme, to remember that idea. Share!

Keeping on Track

If you want to be productive, it can helpful to plan how to handle distractions in advance. Think about the following...

  • What needs to be accomplished
  • How long sessions will be
  • When to have breaks
  • Scheduling something social after studying
  • Think about assigning someone the role of gate-keeper – this person’s job is to make sure the group is on task and to point out when you get off topic
